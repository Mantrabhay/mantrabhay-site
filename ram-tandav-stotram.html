Shri Ram Tandav Stotram

Shri Ram Tandav Stotram is a powerful devotional hymn describing the fierce and divine energy of Lord Rama. Through poetic imagery, it portrays Rama as the destroyer of evil, protector of dharma, and the embodiment of cosmic balance and righteousness.

Meaning of Shri Ram Tandav Stotram

Verse 1

Lord Rama, adorned with divine matted locks and glowing with celestial brilliance, performs His fierce Tandav. His eyes blaze with righteous energy as He embodies both creation and destruction in perfect harmony.

Verse 2

Surrounded by His Vanara and divine allies, Lord Ram becomes the destroyer of evil forces. He is the devourer of darkness and protector of the cosmos, feared by all demonic beings.

Verse 3

Clad in sacred ochre robes and holding His bow, Lord Ram meditates upon truth and justice. Through His Tandav, He shatters sin, illusion, and pride, purifying all existence with divine light.

Verse 4

Through His mighty deeds, Lord Ram tears apart the ignorance that binds creation. Each act reveals His infinite virtues proving that He is beyond mortal comprehension and the master of all dharma.

Verse 5

With His Vanara army beside Him, Lord Ram leaps into battle, destroying demonic legions with divine power. The battlefield becomes sanctified by His courage and righteous energy.

Verse 6

He conquers mighty demons like Kumbhakarna and Ahiravana, shaking the heavens and freeing Lanka. His celestial weapons restore peace and reestablish the glory of dharma.

Verse 7

Praised by sages, yogis, and celestial beings, Lord Ram beloved of Sita destroys Ravana, ending the tyranny of darkness and restoring cosmic order.

Verse 8

In His fierce form, bearing the divine bow, Lord Ram becomes the destroyer of illusion. Worshipped by Vibhishana and revered by all, He embodies truth, strength, and compassion.

Verse 9

Celestial beings circle in awe as Lord Ram performs His Tandav — a cosmic dance that glorifies His lineage and spreads His fame through all realms.

Verse 10

He is formless yet radiant, the eternal creator and destroyer, the Supreme Being who frees devotees from sin, fear, and the cycle of birth and death.

Verse 11

Amidst the chaos of battle, Lord Ram’s divine weapons blaze with power, annihilating the darkness of the demonic hordes. His fury restores balance and peace.

Verse 12

I bow to Lord Ram, beloved of Sita, savior of devotees, and destroyer of sin. His divine grace blesses the world with light, peace, and eternal righteousness.

Conclusion

Thus ends the Shri Ram Tandav Stotram, a hymn glorifying the Supreme Lord Rama whose divine Tandav represents the eternal dance of dharma, destruction of evil, and renewal of cosmic order.
